What's a Knight Tour

A knight's tour is an ancient mathematical problem. In it, a single knight has to visit every square on the chessboard exactly once.

The tours can be closed, meaning that the last knight's move is just one move away from it's initial position. Creating a loop. Or open, in these cases the initial position is out of reach from the knight's last move.

In computer science, there are several ways to find a knight's tour. Using a brute-force search, neural networks,etc. In this case, we'll use what's called Warnsdoff's rule.

Warnsdoff's Rule

Warnsdoff' rule is an heuristic (a technique whose objective is to find and approximate solution in a faster way than other methods) stating that the knight should choose the square with the lowest number of posible moves.
